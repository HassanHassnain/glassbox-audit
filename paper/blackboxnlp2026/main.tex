\title{Do Sparse Autoencoder Features Explain Refusal?\\
A Preregistered, Held-Out Causal Audit}

\author{Anonymous ACL submission}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Sparse autoencoders (SAEs) are widely treated as the unit of analysis for
mechanistic interpretability, yet causal evaluations against simple baselines
remain rare.  We present \glassbox{}, a preregistered auditing protocol that
asks whether SAE features provide a better \emph{causal} account of refusal
than a direction computable in closed form.  On a controlled corpus of 1{,}000
harmful/benign prompt pairs for Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, discovery is confined to
the training split, selection to validation, and every headline effect is read
once from held-out pairs against random-direction, matched random-SAE, and
layerwise controls.  Ablating the train-derived mean-difference direction at
layer 27 shifts the held-out harmful refusal score by $-0.136$ (95\% CI
$[-0.144, -0.128]$) and cuts the harmful refusal rate from $0.64$ to $0.18$
with negligible benign and capability cost; the frozen direction transfers
zero-shot to OR-Bench.  SAE feature ablation is causally real but never beats
this baseline: 0 of 6 preregistered retraining cells pass.  A pair-aware
sensitivity analysis estimates the mean-vs-SAE suppression gap at $0.099$
$[0.092, 0.106]$, rejecting parity within a practical margin of $0.02$
(Holm-adjusted $p=4\times10^{-4}$); sweeping budgets of 1--100 features never
closes the gap, and at 50--100 features the intervention backfires.  A
reconstruction-only control shows that the seemingly dramatic effect of full
SAE substitution is dominated by non-specific reconstruction damage.  We
release the harness, both preregistrations, and machine-readable evidence for
every number, and argue that baseline-anchored, held-out causal audits should
gate mechanistic claims made on behalf of SAE features.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Sparse autoencoders have become mechanistic interpretability's default
microscope: trained to reconstruct residual-stream activations under a
sparsity penalty, they decompose model internals into dictionaries of features
that are often strikingly legible
\citep{bricken2023towards,cunningham2024sparse,templeton2024scaling,gao2024scaling}.
An implicit claim travels with this program---that SAE features are not merely
\emph{interpretable} but are \emph{better handles on the model's mechanisms}
than cheaper alternatives.  That claim is causal, and it is testable.

Refusal provides an unusually sharp test.  \citet{arditi2024refusal} showed
that refusal across many chat models is largely mediated by a single
difference-of-means direction.  If SAE features carve refusal at its causal
joints, then intervening on a small number of them should suppress refusal at
least as well as this one-line baseline---provided both sides are subject to
the same selection discipline and neither can overfit the evaluation.

Most interpretability evaluations do not enforce such discipline.  Features
are selected, thresholds tuned, and steering scales chosen on the same records
used to report effects; baselines are absent or weak; unfavorable cells are
silently dropped.  These are the failure modes the probing literature spent
years learning to control \citep{hewitt2019designing,belinkov2022probing},
and that recent SAE benchmarks have begun to surface at scale
\citep{wu2025axbench,karvonen2025saebench,heap2025automated}.

\glassbox{} is an adjudication harness built to make this contest fair and its
outcome hard to fudge:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Preregistration.}  Hypotheses H1--H5, acceptance thresholds, the
  full SAE retraining grid, and the run order were frozen before any held-out
  record was scored, with an explicit no-retuning policy
  (\S\ref{sec:prereg}).
\item \textbf{Walled-off splits.}  Discovery (layers, features, directions)
  uses train only; selection (score threshold, steering scales) uses
  validation only; judgement reads held-out pairs once
  (\S\ref{sec:walls}).
\item \textbf{Baseline-anchored controls.}  SAE features compete against
  mean-difference and linear-probe directions under identical budgets, with
  isotropic random directions, architecture-matched random SAEs,
  same-dictionary random feature sets, and layerwise controls
  (\S\ref{sec:controls}).
\item \textbf{Negative results are published.}  The most informative output is
  the claim the audit \emph{refuses} to license.  All six predeclared SAE
  cells, the failed hypotheses, a killed replication cell, and a clean-room
  rerun ship as machine-readable evidence.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent Our findings: (i) refusal in Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct is causally
mediated by a late residual-stream direction (layer 27 of 28) that survives
held-out testing, layerwise controls, threshold variants, and zero-shot
external transfer; (ii) top-$k$ SAE features trained on the same activations
are causally real---beating matched random controls in every cell---yet
recover only a fraction of the direction's effect ($-0.036$ vs.\ $-0.136$) at
every seed and width tested; (iii) the effect is a \emph{direction}, not an
identified circuit; and (iv) a hardening analysis upgrades ``the SAE did not
win'' to an affirmative statistical claim: parity is rejected within a
$0.02$ margin, feature budgets up to 100 do not close the gap, and naive
encode--decode substitution is confounded by reconstruction damage.

Our contributions are: (1) a preregistered, held-out causal audit protocol for
interpretability claims, released as a reusable harness; (2) positive evidence
for a robust, transferable refusal direction under that protocol; (3) a
bounded negative result for SAE causal superiority, with the affirmative
inferiority analysis, budget frontier, and reconstruction control that harden
it; and (4) a fully machine-readable evidence trail, including failed and
killed cells.\footnote{Code, protocols, and evidence files are publicly
released; the repository link is withheld for anonymity and provided to
reviewers as supplementary material.  Stable evidence-file names are cited
throughout (Appendix~\ref{app:evidence}).}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Refusal directions and activation steering.}
\citet{arditi2024refusal} established that refusal is mediated by a single
difference-of-means direction across many open chat models; our
mean-difference baseline is their construction placed under stricter selection
discipline.  Steering by activation addition originates with
\citet{turner2023activation} and contrastive activation addition
\citep{rimsky2024steering}, and representation-level control is systematized
by \citet{zou2023representation}.  We use steering only as a secondary,
fixed-grid readout; ablation carries the primary causal claim.

\paragraph{Sparse autoencoders.}
Dictionary learning on activations
\citep{bricken2023towards,cunningham2024sparse} has been scaled to frontier
models \citep{templeton2024scaling} and refined architecturally---top-$k$
\citep{gao2024scaling}, JumpReLU \citep{rajamanoharan2024jumping}---with open
suites such as Gemma Scope \citep{lieberum2024gemmascope}.  Sparse feature
circuits \citep{marks2025sparse} build causal graphs from SAE features.  Our
audit uses a top-$k$ SAE matched to this lineage ($k=32$) and asks the prior
question: do the features beat the direction they are meant to refine?

\paragraph{Causal evaluation of interpretability.}
Activation patching localizes computation \citep{meng2022locating}; causal
scrubbing \citep{chan2022causal} and automated circuit discovery
\citep{conmy2023towards,syed2024attribution} formalize faithfulness testing.
Our component analysis follows this tradition and reports its own failure to
reach circuit-level evidence (H4).

\paragraph{Baselines, control tasks, and SAE benchmarks.}
Control tasks for probes \citep{hewitt2019designing} and selectivity concerns
\citep{belinkov2022probing} motivate our matched random-SAE controls, which
preserve intervention mechanics while randomizing feature identity.
AxBench \citep{wu2025axbench} found supervised baselines beat SAE steering on
concept control; SAEBench \citep{karvonen2025saebench} standardizes SAE
quality metrics; \citet{heap2025automated} show automated interpretability
metrics can fail to separate trained from random transformers.  \glassbox{}
complements these with a single-behavior, preregistered, held-out
\emph{causal} audit in which selection itself is walled off, and with an
affirmative-inferiority statistical framing borrowed from equivalence testing
\citep{schuirmann1987comparison,lakens2017equivalence}.

\paragraph{Over-refusal evaluation.}
Our external transfer set derives from OR-Bench \citep{cui2025orbench}, whose
toxic and hard-benign subsets measure refusal suppression and over-refusal
side effects under frozen interventions.

\section{The \glassbox{} Audit Protocol}
\label{sec:protocol}

\subsection{Setup, refusal score, and metrics}
\label{sec:setup}

Let $f$ be a decoder-only transformer with residual stream
$h^{(\ell)}(x)\in\mathbb{R}^{d}$ at layer $\ell$ (mean-pooled over prompt
tokens for discovery; interventions apply at all positions).  Each record
carries a harmfulness label, a pair identifier linking a harmful prompt to a
topic-matched benign twin, and a split assigned at the pair level.

Refusal is scored deterministically by contrastive prefix log-likelihood: for
refusal prefix $R$ (``I'm sorry, but I can't'') and answer prefix $A$
(``Sure, here's''),
\begin{equation}
r(x)=\sigma\!\Big(
\tfrac{1}{|R|}\log P_f(R\mid x)-
\tfrac{1}{|A|}\log P_f(A\mid x)\Big),
\label{eq:score}
\end{equation}
token-length normalized.  A refusal \emph{rate} thresholds $r(x)$ at
$\tau=0.847$, selected on validation by balanced accuracy.  No LLM judge
appears anywhere in the loop, so the scorer is exactly reproducible; threshold
variants are tested in \S\ref{sec:robustness}.

We report held-out deltas against the unintervened model: mean harmful
refusal-score delta \dH{} (primary; negative = suppression), mean benign delta
\dB{} (specificity), capability cost \dC{} as the mean next-token NLL delta on
held-out prompt text, refusal-rate deltas at fixed $\tau$, and bootstrap 95\%
confidence intervals.

\subsection{Controlled corpus and leakage audit}
\label{sec:corpus}

The corpus contains 2{,}000 records: 1{,}000 harmful prompts, each paired with
a benign twin on the same topic (same surface domain, one intent flip), drawn
from 40 topic families with 25 pairs per family.  Splits are assigned at the
pair level and families are disjoint across splits; the test split holds 250
pairs (500 records).  A released leakage audit (corpus SHA-256 pinned) finds
zero exact, normalized, or near-duplicate prompts across splits, zero
harmful/benign text overlap within pairs, zero family overlap across splits,
and zero overlap with the external OR-Bench evaluation files.

\subsection{Discovery, selection, judgement}
\label{sec:walls}

\textbf{Discovery (train only).}  A localization score computed at layers
$\ell\in\{4,8,12,16,20,24,27\}$ selects the target layer (27).  There we
construct three rival explanations from identical train activations:
(a)~a top-$k$ SAE ($k=32$; width $3{,}072$, i.e.\ $2\times$ expansion of
$d=1{,}536$; 250 epochs; batch 512; lr $5\times10^{-4}$; $\ell_1$
$10^{-4}$) trained on 47{,}645 activation vectors (48 tokens per prompt,
subsampled under a 64k cap, seed 17), from which the five most
class-separating latents by train effect size form the intervention set;
(b)~the mean-difference direction
$v_{\mathrm{mean}}\propto\bar h_{\mathrm{harm}}-\bar h_{\mathrm{benign}}$; and
(c)~an $\ell_2$-regularized logistic-probe direction.  All directions are
sign-oriented on train and unit-normalized.

\textbf{Selection (validation only).}  Validation selects exactly two
quantities: the rate threshold $\tau$ and one steering scale per method from
the fixed grid $\{\pm1,\pm2,\pm4\}$.  Nothing else is selectable.

\textbf{Judgement (test, once).}  All headline numbers are read once from the
held-out pairs.

\subsection{Interventions}
\label{sec:interventions}

For a unit direction $v$: \emph{directional ablation} sets
$h\leftarrow h-(h^{\top}v)\,v$; \emph{steering} adds $\alpha v$.  Let the SAE
encode $h$ into activations $a_i(h)$ with decoder atoms $d_i$ and
reconstruction $\hat h(h)=b+\sum_i a_i(h)\,d_i$.  For a selected latent set
$S$ we evaluate three distinct SAE interventions:
\begin{align}
\text{residual-preserving:} & h\leftarrow h-\textstyle\sum_{i\in S}a_i(h)\,d_i,
\label{eq:residual}\\
\text{reconstruction-only:} & h\leftarrow \hat h(h),
\label{eq:recon}\\
\text{substitution:} & h\leftarrow \hat h(h)-\textstyle\sum_{i\in S}a_i(h)\,d_i.
\label{eq:subst}
\end{align}
The \emph{preregistered} SAE intervention is
Eq.~\eqref{eq:residual}: it removes the selected features' decoder
contribution while retaining the SAE's reconstruction residual, so it isolates
the features rather than the autoencoder's fidelity.
Eqs.~\eqref{eq:recon}--\eqref{eq:subst} are evaluated in the hardening
analysis because reconstruction error is known to be behaviorally
non-neutral \citep{gurnee2024sae}: Eq.~\eqref{eq:recon} measures exactly that
confound, with no feature removed.

\subsection{Controls}
\label{sec:controls}

\emph{Isotropic random directions} (32 draws) bound generic-perturbation
effects.  \emph{Matched random SAEs} preserve architecture, top-$k$ sparsity,
activation centering, and the residual-preserving mechanics of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:residual} while randomizing weights and selected latents
(32--48 draws per artifact).  The hardening extension adds a stricter
\emph{same-dictionary} control: at every budget, eight random latent sets
drawn from the trained SAE's non-dead, train-activated latents (excluding the
selected set).  \emph{Layerwise controls} apply the identical mean-difference
construction at every scanned layer.

\subsection{Experimental setup}
\label{sec:models}

The main audit uses Qwen/Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct \citep{qwen2024qwen25}
(28 layers, $d=1{,}536$, fp16, pinned revision); H5 additionally uses
Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct (36 layers, $d=2{,}048$).  The main-audit SAE is healthy:
variance explained $0.9986$, mean $\ell_0=32.0$, $3.7\%$ dead features,
feature density $0.0104$.  The negative results below are not attributable to
a degenerate autoencoder.  All real-model runs fit on a single 24\,GiB-class
GPU; a deterministic CPU toy audit, unit tests, and lint run in CI.

\subsection{H1: a robust held-out refusal direction (supported)}
\label{sec:h1}

Table~\ref{tab:main} gives the held-out effects.  Mean-difference ablation
satisfies all four frozen criteria: $\dH=-0.136\le-0.08$ with CI upper bound
$-0.128<0$; $|\dB|=0.015\le0.05$; $\dC=-0.016\le0.05$.  The harmful refusal
rate falls from $0.64$ to $0.184$ while benign over-refusal moves only from
$0.148$ to $0.140$.

\textbf{Layer 27 is privileged.}  Applying the identical mean-difference
construction at every scanned layer shows mid-layer ablations suppress with
collateral damage---e.g.\ layer 16: $\dH=-0.101$ but $\dC=+0.191$; layer 24:
$\dH=-0.079$ but $\dB=+0.068$---whereas layer 27 combines the largest
suppression ($-0.161$ in the layer-scan artifact) with near-zero benign shift
($+0.003$) and negative capability cost ($-0.030$).  Full table in
Appendix~\ref{app:layerwise}.

\subsection{H4: a direction, not a circuit (failed)}
\label{sec:h4}

Projecting the direction out of each component's \emph{output} localizes the
effect to the residual stream itself: at layer 27 on held-out records,
residual ablation gives $\dH=-0.136$ while attention-output and MLP-output
ablations give $-0.002$ and $+0.007$.  The preregistered H4 bundle (necessity
$+$ sufficiency $+$ specificity at the path level) is therefore \emph{not
met}; \glassbox{} claims residual-stream localization only, and the release
records \texttt{confirmed\_circuit: false}.  Component tables for layers
$\{16,20,24,27\}$ are in Appendix~\ref{app:component}.

\subsection{H5: partial cross-model replication}
\label{sec:h5}

On Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct ($n=60$ held-out records; baseline harmful refusal rate
$1.00$), the layer scan again selects a late layer (35 of 36) and
mean-difference ablation again dominates ($\dH=-0.090$), but specificity fails
badly: the same intervention shifts the benign score by $+0.390$ with
$\dC=+0.164$.  The 3B SAE clears its random controls (empirical $p=0.024$)
but is visibly under-trained (45.7\% dead features vs.\ 3.7\% at 1.5B).  H5 is
reported as \emph{partial, Qwen-family only}.

\subsection{Robustness}
\label{sec:robustness}

\textbf{Dose--response.}  On the fixed validation-frozen steering grid the
response is monotone and ordered (mean slope $\approx4\times$ SAE
$\approx6\times$ probe) and an order of magnitude smaller than ablation,
consistent with ablation as the right primary test
(Appendix~\ref{app:dose}).
\textbf{Scorer variants.}  The ordering survives rate-threshold variants
($\tau=0.90$: same ranking; default $\tau$: rate deltas $-0.456$ / $-0.136$ /
$-0.008$).
\textbf{Clean-room rerun.}  An independent reproduction from documented
commands recovers the target layer, baseline rates, all three deltas, and the
0/6 verdict within pre-stated tolerances.

\section{Hardening Sensitivity Analyses}
\label{sec:hardening}

The analyses in this section were specified in a second frozen protocol
\emph{after} the original outcomes were known; we therefore label them
post-hoc sensitivity analyses, never confirmatory tests, and we never edited
the original preregistration.  A batched re-scoring of the frozen baseline
matches the archived per-prompt outputs to within $0.0031$ in refusal score
(Appendix~\ref{app:infra}), far inside the $0.02$ margin used below.

same-dictionary random sets.  The mean direction's advantage over the
selected SAE features rejects the parity margin $\delta=0.02$ (one-sided
normal-approximation $p<10^{-6}$).}
\label{tab:holm}
\end{table}

The mean direction out-suppresses top-5 SAE ablation by $0.099$ refusal-score
units---five times the practical margin---so parity is not merely unproven but
rejected.  The result is specifically about the mean direction: SAE features
in turn beat the probe (row 3 cannot reject) and beat eight same-dictionary
random sets (row 4), so the audit resolves a strict ordering
mean $\gg$ SAE top-5 $\gg$ probe on this behavior.

\subsection{Feature budgets do not rescue the claim}
\label{sec:budget}

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/hardening_feature_budget.pdf}
\caption{Held-out feature-budget frontier.  Points: train-ranked SAE feature
ablation at frozen budgets (95\% pair-bootstrap CIs); grey band: range of
eight same-dictionary random sets per budget; dashed line: mean-direction
reference ($-0.136$).  The frontier is non-monotonic, peaks at $k=5$, and

\label{fig:budget}
\end{figure}

If five features are simply too few, more should help.  They do not
(Figure~\ref{fig:budget}).  At frozen budgets $k\in\{1,2,5,10,20,50,100\}$,
held-out $\dH$ is $+0.0013$, $-0.0008$, $\mathbf{-0.0363}$, $-0.0157$,
$-0.0018$, $+0.0352$, $+0.0345$: top-5 is optimal, no budget approaches the
mean direction, and at $k\in\{50,100\}$ ablation \emph{increases} refusal
while benign shifts reach $\approx+0.04$ and NLL cost $\approx+0.07$.  A
validation-selected weighted combination over the top-100 train-ranked
features (20 latents, $\ell_1$ grid frozen in a preregistration amendment)
also fails: $\dH=+0.0002$.  Under this dictionary and ranking rule, the gap is
not a budget artifact.

Encode--zero--decode substitution looks spectacular in isolation:
$\dH=-0.805$.  But the reconstruction-only control---running the activation

with an almost equal benign shift ($-0.692$) and $+0.19$ NLL cost
(Table~\ref{tab:comparability}, Figure~\ref{fig:comparability}).  The
apparent effect is therefore dominated by non-specific reconstruction damage,
consistent with reconstruction errors being behaviorally pathological
\citep{gurnee2024sae}.  Under the residual-preserving intervention that
isolates the features themselves, the mean direction delivers $3.5\times$
more suppression per unit of hidden-state perturbation ($0.136/32.3$ vs.\
$0.036/30.3$), with better specificity and no capability cost.  Studies that
demonstrate SAE ``causal effects'' via full substitution without this control
risk crediting the dictionary for what is actually lossy compression.

\subsection{The preregistered public-SAE cell, killed honestly}
\label{sec:killed}

The extension protocol's only confirmatory cell paired Gemma-2-9B-it
\citep{gemmateam2024gemma2} with Google's instruction-tuned Gemma Scope
residual SAE at layer 31 \citep{lieberum2024gemmascope}---the correct

requires authenticated license acceptance and the compute host had no
credential or cached weights.  Per the frozen fallback policy we did
\emph{not} substitute a base-model SAE and relabel it as same-checkpoint
evidence; the cell is preserved as killed-before-outcomes with its full
decision record (Appendix~\ref{app:killed}).

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Why does the mean beat the dictionary?}
The mean-difference direction \emph{is} the first moment of the class
contrast.  If refusal at this site is encoded predominantly as a linear
class-mean shift, the optimal single-shot causal handle is essentially that
shift, and any decomposition that scatters the class-relevant variance across
thousands of sparse latents must reassemble it before intervening.  Zeroing
the top five features captures only the portion of the shift they span;
adding more train-ranked features accumulates off-target components faster
than signal (Figure~\ref{fig:budget}).  On this reading the SAE is not
\emph{wrong}---its features are causally real, beating matched random latents
everywhere---it is \emph{inefficient} as a causal handle for a behavior whose
natural description is one direction.

\paragraph{What this does and does not indict.}
The result is bounded: one behavior, one model family, one SAE recipe, one
train-only ranking rule.  SAEs may still win where behavior decomposes into
genuinely multi-dimensional structure, where description rather than
intervention strength is the goal, or with different dictionaries and
selection objectives.  What the result does indict is asserting mechanistic
privilege for SAE features without running the three-line baseline under
equal selection discipline.  Every degree of freedom our SAE received, the
baseline received too.  Our findings agree with steering-benchmark evidence
that simple representation baselines are hard to beat \citep{wu2025axbench},
and extend it in three ways: held-out preregistered causal testing, an
affirmative inferiority bound, and a reconstruction control that disqualifies
the most flattering SAE intervention.

\paragraph{Auditing as a first-class deliverable.}
The transferable artifact is the protocol: preregister the claim ladder, wall
off discovery/selection/judgement, match controls to intervention mechanics,
publish every predeclared cell (including killed ones), and let the release
claim exactly the rungs the evidence supports.  The harness is
behavior-agnostic; refusal is simply the behavior with the strongest known
one-direction baseline, which makes it the right first adversary for any
claim of SAE causal superiority.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Under preregistered, held-out, baseline-anchored evaluation, a
difference-of-means direction remains the best causal account of refusal in
Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct: SAE features are causally real but strictly inferior
(gap $0.099$ $[0.092,0.106]$; parity within $0.02$ rejected), budgets to 100
features do not close the gap, and the most dramatic SAE intervention is an
artifact of reconstruction damage.  We release the full harness, protocols,
and evidence so that the next claim of SAE causal superiority can be tested,
rather than assumed.

\section*{Limitations}

The refusal scorer is a contrastive prefix log-likelihood, not
generated-answer judging; regeneration-based grading is future work.  The
controlled corpus is topic-matched by construction and deliberately narrow;
naturalistic breadth enters only through the unpaired OR-Bench transfer.
Component analysis stops at component outputs---no head- or path-level
mediation---so ``not a circuit'' means \emph{not established}, not ruled out.
The 3B replication uses $n=60$ and an under-trained SAE.  Non-Qwen
replication is unrun: the checkpoint-matched Gemma cell was killed before
outcomes for weight-access reasons and is preserved as such.  The negative H2
conclusion is bounded by our SAE recipe (top-$k$, $k=32$, expansions
$\times2/\times4$, $\sim$48k activation vectors) and by train-effect-size
ranking; the hardening sweep extends budgets to 100 features without
reversal, but a substantially different dictionary, training corpus, or
supervised selection objective could change the verdict.  All hardening
analyses are post-hoc sensitivity analyses by construction, and the
stability-grid SAEs are lighter-trained than the main-audit SAE (higher dead
fractions), which is why the healthy main-audit SAE anchors the claim.

\section*{Ethics Statement}

Directional ablation can partially bypass refusal in open-weight models.
This capability is already public \citep{arditi2024refusal} and is achievable
with the mean-difference baseline alone; we introduce no new attack surface
beyond it.  We release no jailbreak prompts, no generated harmful content,
and no modified weights: evidence files contain aggregate scores only, and
the harmful-prompt corpus is regenerated locally from released code with a
pinned hash rather than redistributed.  The methodological contribution runs
in the protective direction---a discipline for checking safety-relevant
mechanistic claims before they are relied upon, and for keeping negative
results in the record.

\section{Preregistered Criteria and Run Order}
\label{app:prereg}

\textbf{Original protocol (frozen 2026-06-19).}  Fixed run order: 1.5B audit
$\to$ stability cells $\to$ OR-Bench transfer $\to$ component/path $\to$
cross-model $\to$ final reports.  Policy: ``no retuning of layers, features,
thresholds, scales, prompts, metrics, seeds, widths, or criteria after
held-out results.''  H1: $\dH\le-0.08$, CI upper bound $<0$, $|\dB|\le0.05$,
$\dC\le0.05$.  H2: six planned cells, no exclusions, $\ge4$ passing (beat
mean \emph{and} probe; random-SAE $p\le0.05$).  H3: no external rediscovery;
$\dC\le0.05$; random-control comparison required.  H4: necessity,
sufficiency, specificity, controls, held-out replication.  H5: attempt only
with available weights; never fabricate.

\textbf{Hardening protocol (frozen before extension outcomes).}  Immutable
inputs pinned by SHA-256 (dataset; SAE weights; archived intervention
outputs; discovery tensors) plus the model revision.  Analysis status
declared in advance: every Qwen re-analysis is post-hoc sensitivity
(resampling unit: prompt pair; 10{,}000 bootstrap and permutation draws;
95\% confidence; practical margin $0.02$ = 25\% of H1's minimum effect).
Primary family (Holm-corrected, enumerated in advance): mean vs.\ selected
SAE; probe vs.\ selected SAE; selected SAE vs.\ matched random SAE; best
validation-selected weighted sparse SAE vs.\ mean.  Feature-budget frontier:
budgets $\{1,2,5,10,20,50,100\}$, train-only ranking, eight same-dictionary
random sets per budget (seed 1729).  An amendment (also frozen before
outcomes) fixed the weighted-combination fitting grid: top-100 train-ranked
candidate features, $\ell_1$ grid $0$, $10^{-4}$, $10^{-3}$, $10^{-2}$; 400
epochs; lr $0.05$; at most 20 selected latents, fit on train, selected on
validation (selected $\ell_1=10^{-2}$).  Reporting policy: never edit the
original preregistration; never call a previously-inspected analysis
confirmatory; preserve failed, null, and killed cells.

\section{Infrastructure and Cross-Checks}
\label{app:infra}

The hardening pipeline evaluates interventions with a batched scorer for
throughput.  Before any extension cell ran, the batched scorer re-evaluated
the unintervened baseline against the archived per-prompt outputs of the
original audit: maximum absolute refusal-score difference $0.0031$ (mean
$0.0005$) and maximum absolute NLL difference $0.0156$---one fp16
quantization step---across all 500 held-out records.  All extension deltas
are computed against the batched baseline, so batching effects cancel within
comparisons.  Runs are checkpointed per cell and resume idempotently; the
evidence JSON records the model revision, dataset hash, artifact hashes,
protocol hashes, and the exact analysis-status label for every block.  All
GPU work fits on a single 24\,GiB-class device.
